Information Technology and Web Science

Graduate Information Technology and Web Science Program

Interdisciplinary From Day One. In a world in which information technology affects virtually everything we do, there is a demand for an academic program that offers students the deepest understanding of the Web. As an ITWS student, you will investigate issues related to security, trust, privacy, content value, and the development of the Web in the future.

About the Program

You will take a core set of courses on Web and IT development and management, along with courses in your focus track, selected from more than 20 options, including arts, engineering, management, communications, artificial intelligence, Web systems, and medicine.

Curriculum

Completion of the M.S. in Information Technology and Web Science requires a total of 30 - 36 credit hours.

  • Core Courses (12 credits)
  • Elective Courses (15 - 18 credits)
  • Thesis or Project (6 credits)

Our program emphasizes cutting-edge research in areas such as web technologies, data analytics, and cybersecurity. Students have the opportunity to collaborate on research projects with faculty.

Ph.D. in Multidisciplinary Science

Ph.D. programs in Architecture, Engineering, Humanities and Social Sciences, Management, and Science offer a wealth of opportunities for doctoral study with focused IT research.

In addition, for highly interdisciplinary research, an IT Track is available in the Multidisciplinary Science Ph.D. program. The IT Track is designed to allow a student to work with faculty from multiple Rensselaer departments and schools.

Information Technology Track

The IT Track in the Multidisciplinary Science PhD program is designed to prepare students for a career in interdisciplinary IT research. The IT Track requires a technical core coupled with a concentration that provides the context for the student's research. This concentration may incorporate one or more disciplines in addition to its core IT components.
